Course Overview

Today’s world requires both technical and soft skills for specialised positions which includes the role of a Scrum Master.

The Scrum Master serves to facilitate scrum to the larger team by ensuring the scrum framework is followed. He or she is committed to the scrum values, agile principles and best practices. The scrum master should also remain flexible and open to opportunities for the team to improve their workflow.

In this training, we will teach you how to use project management tools, collaboration tools, capacity planning, and agile games to improve team performance, and conflict management. We will also help with interview preparation.

Scrum Master Accountability

  • As a Scrum Master, you are accountable for the overall effectiveness of the team by enabling the teams improve its practices, within the scrum framework
  • Serves the team by facilitating removal of impediments to progress and helping them to focus on creating high-value increment.
  • Facilitating stakeholder collaboration as requested or needed.
  • Accountable for establishing Scrum as defined by the Scrum Guide
  • Coaches the team and the entire organization on Scrum adoption, principles and practices, self-management, and cross-functionality.
  • Facilitates scrum events, ensuring they take place, positive, productive, and kept within the timebox.
  • Helping the Scrum Team understand the need for clear and concise Product Backlog items.
  • Helping find techniques for effective Product Goal definition and Product Backlog Management.
  • The Scrum Master removes conflicts within the team and fosters an environment where team members feel psychologically safe.
  • Helping employees and stakeholders understand and enact an empirical approach for complex work.
  • Removing barriers between the scrum teams and stakeholders.

Agile Scrum Ceremonies

Sprint Planning:This is the meeting for the team to consider items of work to be completed and commits to what they have capacity to deliver during the coming sprint

Daily Stand-Up:This is the team meeting to provide update and progress of individual work done towards adding value to product development

Scrum Review:This is where the team showcases what they have completed for the stakeholders review and feedback and get a sign off

Scrum Retrospective:This a meeting where the team reflects on their performance during the current sprint seek ways to improve their interraction, practices, and lessons learnt

Sprint:This is a cycle of work for the team (can be 1-4 weeks interval)
